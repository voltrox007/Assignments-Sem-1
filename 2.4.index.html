Calisthenics is a type of exercise that uses one’s own body weight as resistance to develop strength, endurance, and mobility. It is a form of resistance training that does not require any external equipment, making it accessible and convenient for anyone, anywhere.

Characteristics:

Calisthenics exercises typically involve:

Bodyweight movements: Squats, lunges, push-ups, pull-ups, dips, and other exercises that engage multiple muscle groups.

Gravity-based resistance: The weight of the body provides resistance, challenging muscles and improving strength.

Functional movements: Calisthenics exercises often mimic everyday activities, such as climbing, jumping, or crawling, which helps improve coordination and overall fitness.

Modifiable: Exercises can be adjusted to suit different fitness levels, ages, and abilities.

Low-impact: Calisthenics can be a low-impact option for those who want to reduce joint stress or avoid high-impact activities.
